BAAL — Pre-Expansion Priority Assessment
==========================================
Generated 2026-03-03

What's Strong (Leave Alone)
---------------------------
- God system — The standout feature. 8 distinct gods, meaningful gifts, apostasy
  consequences. DCSS-quality design.
- Monster variety — 63 monsters with real mechanical differences (mimics,
  doppelgangers, faction neutrality, confusion auras). Solid.
- Core loop — Movement, combat, permadeath, ghost system, the Rune escape
  mechanic. All feel good.
- UI — Side panel, log, modals, look mode, minimap. Clean and functional.


What Needs Work Before New Content
----------------------------------

1. ITEMS ARE TOO LINEAR
   Every weapon/armor slot is just "find bigger number." No interesting
   trade-offs — no light weapon that attacks twice, no heavy armor that slows
   you, no cursed sword with a powerful upside. Before adding new classes/races
   that need distinct gear, the item system needs depth.
   Ideas:
   - Unique/named items (Sword of Kas, Staff of the Magi-type things)
   - Weapon properties beyond raw damage (reach, speed, lifesteal, elemental)
   - Meaningful cursed items (powerful but with a cost, not just "gotcha" traps)

2. SPELLS LACK TACTICAL DEPTH
   Magic-User has 8 spells but they're basically "small damage, medium damage,
   big damage, huge damage." Cleric has 5, mostly healing. Before adding a 4th
   class, the spell system needs more variety — buffs, debuffs, terrain
   manipulation, utility spells (knock, detect monsters, create light). Right
   now there's no reason to cast Magic Missile when you have a Fire Wand.

3. DUNGEON FLOORS ALL FEEL THE SAME
   Every floor is the same BSP rectangle soup. Before adding sub-levels/branches,
   the base dungeon needs more variety:
   - Themed floor aesthetics (crypt floors, flooded caves, lava forge)
   - Special room types (treasure vaults, libraries, armories)
   - Floor-specific mechanics (darkness floors where FOV is halved, flooded
     floors with water movement penalties)
   - Pre-built "vault" templates mixed into the BSP generation

4. WANDS ARE OVERPOWERED / SPELLS ARE UNDERPOWERED
   A Fire Wand (4d6, no MP cost, 5-10 charges) outclasses most spells. Fireball
   costs 14 MP for 8d6 — better damage but you get maybe 2-3 casts. Wands
   trivialize mid-game combat. Either nerf wand damage, make them rarer, or buff
   spell utility to compensate.

5. COMPANION SYSTEM IS SHALLOW
   Companions are basically turrets that follow you. They don't level up, can't
   equip gear, have no dialogue beyond hire/insult, and don't react to events.
   Before adding content that interacts with companions (new races with new
   hostility dynamics, sub-levels with companion-specific events), they need
   more depth:
   - Leveling or scaling with the player
   - Reactions to gods, floors, bosses
   - Equipment slots (even just weapon + armor)

6. STATUS EFFECTS NEED COUNTERPLAY
   Once you're poisoned or paralyzed, you just wait it out. No antidote potions
   (Clarity cures everything but is rare), no cleric "cure poison" spell, no
   resistance mechanic that reduces duration. The player has very few ways to
   inflict statuses either — almost entirely reactive. Needs:
   - Cure-specific potions or spells
   - Player-inflictable statuses via melee (poisoned blade, stunning blow)
   - Resistance stat or saving throws each turn to shake effects early

7. EARLY GAME IS TOO QUIET
   Floor 1 has no shops, no NPCs, and weak monsters. Floors 1-4 feel like a
   warm-up rather than a challenge. The game doesn't start making interesting
   decisions until floor 5+. Consider front-loading some interesting choices
   (an early NPC encounter, a guaranteed shrine on floor 2, etc.).

8. NO-GOD BUILDS ARE DEAD
   Refusing all gods means no active abilities, no gifts, no piety bonuses.
   There's no "atheist" path with its own rewards. This limits build variety.


Priority Order
--------------
Tackle these before adding new classes, races, and sublevels:

  1. Item depth (unique items, weapon properties, meaningful trade-offs)
  2. Dungeon variety (themed floors, vaults, special rooms)
  3. Spell system expansion (utility spells, buffs/debuffs, tactical options)
  4. Wand/spell balance pass
  5. Status effect counterplay (cures, resistances, player-inflicted statuses)
  6. Companion depth (leveling, reactions, basic equipment)

These build the foundation that new classes, races, and sublevels will plug
into. Adding a Rogue class is meaningless if there are no traps to create, no
stealth mechanics, and no daggers with special properties. Adding a Crypt
sub-level is pointless if it looks and plays like every other floor.
